TP1. LO 6.1 — Trade Discounts Research

Response Outline

Explain seller incentives (volume, loyalty) and buyer benefit (lower cost). Compare which is better for buyer depending on size/terms.

TP2. LO 6.2 — Perpetual vs Periodic Choice

Prompt Evaluate perpetual vs periodic for a small convenience store: benefits/challenges, example transaction under each, financial statement impacts, and choose the best fit.

Response Outline

Perpetual: real-time inventory/COGS, better shrink control, needs scanners/software. Periodic: lower cost, simpler, but stale inventory/COGS until count. Example purchase entry differs (Inventory vs Purchases). Perpetual yields timely gross margin; periodic updates at count. Small store may start periodic if budget-limited; scale to perpetual as volume grows.

TP3. LO 6.5 — Credit Invoice Design

Prompt Create a sample credit sales invoice (company info, date, customer, item, price/units, terms, shipping, contract language) and reflect on format/terms/pricing vs peers.

Response Outline

Include header/logo, invoice/date/ship-to, item lines, subtotals, freight, terms (e.g., 2/10, n/30; FOB), warranties/returns limits. Reflection: why chosen terms, risk mitigation, competitive pricing vs peers.

TP4. LO 6.6 — Public Company Income Statement

Prompt Review a public company’s recent income statement: format, contents, pros/cons, and compute Gross Profit Margin.

Response Outline

Identify company and format (multi-step common). Note line items (net sales, COGS, gross profit, opex, other). Discuss suitability and limitations. Compute GPM = Gross Profit / Net Sales; interpret trend vs peers.

TP5. LO 6.7 — Periodic Inventory in Clothing

Prompt Research clothing industry inventory methods; periodic vs perpetual; pros/cons; other businesses using periodic.

Response Outline

Many apparel retailers use perpetual with POS; smaller boutiques may use periodic. Periodic pros: low tech cost; cons: poor shrink/real-time data. Advantages: simplicity, fewer system needs; disadvantages: stale COGS, harder control. Other periodic users: small gift shops, seasonal vendors, low-volume artisans.

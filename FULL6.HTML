Short Answer (SA 1–25)

Concept checks, ratio work, and side-by-side system comparisons.

  • SA 1. What are some benefits to a retailer for offering a discount to a customer?
    Answer: Discounts speed up cash collection, reduce credit risk, strengthen liquidity, and encourage repeat purchases.
  • SA 2. What do credit terms of 4/10, n/30 mean in regard to a purchase?
    Answer: The buyer receives a 4% discount if payment is made within 10 days; otherwise the full amount is due in 30 days.
  • SA 3. What is the difference between a sales return and a sales allowance?
    Answer: A sales return involves physically returning goods; a sales allowance is a price reduction for goods the customer keeps.
  • SA 4. If a retailer made a $350 purchase with terms 2/15, n/60, what would be paid if the discount is taken?
    Answer: Discount = 2% × 350 = $7. Cash paid = $343.
  • SA 5. What are two advantages and disadvantages of the perpetual inventory system?
    Answer: Advantages: real-time accuracy, better shrinkage control. Disadvantages: higher cost to implement, increased tech requirements.
  • SA 6. What are two advantages and disadvantages of the periodic inventory system?
    Answer: Advantages: inexpensive, simple. Disadvantages: no real-time inventory, COGS only known at period-end.
  • SA 7. Sunrise Flowers sells flowers on credit for $130; cost is $50. What is the sale entry under a periodic system?
    Answer: Debit Accounts Receivable $130; Credit Sales $130. No cost entry under periodic.
  • SA 8. Sunrise Flowers sells flowers on credit for $130; cost is $50. What is the sale entry under a perpetual system?
    Answer: Debit AR $130; Credit Sales $130. Debit COGS $50; Credit Merchandise Inventory $50.
  • SA 9. Name two situations where cash would be remitted to a retailer from a manufacturer after purchase.
    Answer: A purchase return after payment, or a purchase allowance issued after payment.
  • SA 10. Retailer buys $750, returns $30, gets $95 allowance, terms 2/10. What is the discount?
    Answer: Net amount = 750 − 30 − 95 = $625. Discount = 2% × 625 = $12.50.
  • SA 11. 50% of $1,100 damaged. Retailer returns 35% of damaged items. Value returned?
    Answer: Damaged = $550. Returned = 35% × 550 = $110.
  • SA 12. Name two situations where cash would be remitted to a customer after purchase.
    Answer: A sales return after payment or a sales allowance issued after payment.
  • SA 13. Customer buys $340, returns $70, gets $65 allowance, terms 3/10. Discount?
    Answer: Net amount = 340 − 70 − 65 = $205. Discount = 3% × 205 = $6.15.
  • SA 14. 60% of $3,600 damaged. Customer returns 35% of damaged items. Value returned?
    Answer: Damaged = $2,160. Returned = 35% × 2,160 = $756.
  • SA 15. What are the main differences between FOB Destination and FOB Shipping Point?
    Answer: Shipping Point: buyer owns goods in transit, buyer pays freight, transfer at seller’s dock. Destination: seller owns goods in transit, seller pays freight, transfer at buyer’s location.
  • SA 16. Buyer purchases $250 on credit, shipping $50, FOB Destination. What entries?
    Answer: Buyer only records the purchase: Debit Inventory (or Purchases) $250; Credit AP $250. No freight entry for buyer.
  • SA 17. Seller sells $800 on credit, cost $300, shipping $100, FOB Destination. What entries?
    Answer: Debit AR $800; Credit Sales $800. Debit COGS $300; Credit Inventory $300. Debit Freight-Out $100; Credit Cash $100.
  • SA 18. On which statement and where is freight-out recorded? Why?
    Answer: Freight-out appears on the income statement under operating (selling) expenses because it is a delivery cost, not part of inventory cost.
  • SA 19. Sunrise Motors: Sales 256,400; SR&A 34,890; Discounts 44,760; COGS 120,470. Gross Profit Margin Ratio?
    Answer: Net sales = 176,750. Gross profit = 56,280. GPM ≈ 32%.
  • SA 20. Difference between a multi-step and simple income statement?
    Answer: Multi-step separates gross profit, operating income, and non-operating items. Simple combines all revenues and all expenses with no subtotals.
  • SA 21. How can investors or lenders use Gross Profit Margin?
    Answer: GPM reveals pricing power and cost control; stronger margins indicate better ability to cover expenses, generate profit, and repay debt.
  • SA 22. August Sundries: Gross margin using multi-step format?
    Answer: Net sales = 469,990. Gross margin = 169,150.
  • SA 23. Retailer buys $680, returns $120, allowance $70, terms 3/10. Discount?
    Answer: Net amount = 680 − 120 − 70 = 490. Discount = 3% × 490 = $14.70.
  • SA 24. 50% of $5,950 damaged. Customer returns 40% of damaged portion. Value returned?
    Answer: Damaged = 2,975. Returned = 40% × 2,975 = 1,190.
  • SA 25. Difference in reporting sellable returns under perpetual vs periodic systems?
    Answer: Perpetual updates inventory and COGS immediately; periodic records only the sales return and adjusts inventory/COGS at period-end.

  • EA1. Bates Board Shop sells 300 surfboards at $400; cost $140; terms 3/15, n/30, dated March 1. A. Initial sale
    B. Payment on March 10.
    Answer:
    Sales = 300 × 400 = 120,000; Cost = 300 × 140 = 42,000.

    A. March 1
    Debit Accounts Receivable $120,000
    Credit Sales $120,000
    Debit Cost of Goods Sold $42,000
    Credit Merchandise Inventory $42,000

    B. March 10 (within discount)
    Discount = 3% × 120,000 = 3,600; Cash = 116,400.
    Debit Cash $116,400
    Debit Sales Discounts $3,600
    Credit Accounts Receivable $120,000
  • EA2. Marx Corp. purchases 135 fax machines on credit at $250, terms 4/10, n/20; pays April 17.
    Answer:

    Credit Accounts Payable $33,750

    Payment within discount

    Credit Merchandise Inventory $1,350

  • EA3. Match sales/purchase terms with definitions (Sales allowance, Purchase return, etc.).
    Answer:
    A. Sales allowance → iii. Customer gets partial refund, keeps goods.
    B. Purchase return → vi. Retailer returns merchandise for full refund.
    C. Sales discount → iv. Customer pays in full within discount window.
    D. Purchase discount → vii. Retailer pays in full within discount window.
    E. Sales return → i. Customer returns merchandise for full refund.
    F. Trade discount → v. Negotiated discount before final price.
    G. Purchase allowance → ii. Retailer gets partial refund, keeps goods.
  • EA4. Mars Corp. — compute net purchases and COGS for March (data from text).
    Answer: Use:
    Net Purchases = Purchases − Purchase Returns − Purchase Allowances − Purchase Discounts + Freight-In
    COG Available = Beginning Inventory + Net Purchases
    COGS = COG Available − Ending Inventory
  • EA5. Barrio Bikes — customer returns 20 bikes ($250 sale, $100 cost). Customer hasn’t paid; bikes are sellable. Record under: A. Perpetual

1. LO 6.1
What are some benefits to a retailer for offering a discount to a customer?
It helps solidify a long-term relationship with the customer, encourages the customer to purchase more, and decreases the time it takes for the company to see a liquid asset (cash).
2. LO 6.1
What do credit terms of 4/10, n/30 mean in regard to a purchase?
A retailer will receive a 4% discount on their purchase if they pay within 10 days, or they have a total of 30 days to pay in full without receiving a discount.
3. LO 6.1
What is the difference between a sales return and a sales allowance?
A sales return occurs when a customer returns merchandise for a full refund. A sales allowance occurs when a customer keeps the merchandise (often because it is slightly defective) and is issued a partial refund.
4. LO 6.1
If a retailer made a purchase in the amount of $350 with credit terms of 2/15, n/60. What would the retailer pay in cash if they received the discount?
$343. ($350 - ($350 * 0.02) = $343).
5. LO 6.2
What are two advantages and disadvantages of the perpetual inventory system?
Advantages: Real-time data and information for decision making. Disadvantages: Can be costly to implement and time-consuming to maintain.
6. LO 6.2
What are two advantages and disadvantages of the periodic inventory system?
Advantages: Cost-effective and easy to maintain. Disadvantages: No real-time data for managers; shrinkage is harder to detect until the end-of-period count.
7. LO 6.2
Sunrise Flowers sells flowers to a customer on credit for $130 on October 18, with a cost of sale to Sunrise of $50. What entry to recognize this sale is required if Sunrise Flowers uses a periodic inventory system?
Debit Accounts Receivable $130, Credit Sales $130. (No COGS entry).
8. LO 6.2
Sunrise Flowers sells flowers to a customer on credit for $130 on October 18, with a cost of sale to Sunrise of $50. What entry to recognize this sale is required if Sunrise Flowers uses a perpetual inventory system?
Debit AR $130 / Credit Sales $130; Debit COGS $50 / Credit Inventory $50.
9. LO 6.3
Name two situations where cash would be remitted to a retailer from a manufacturer after purchase.
1) Return of merchandise after payment. 2) Allowance granted after payment.
10. LO 6.3
If a retailer purchased inventory in the amount of $750, terms 2/10, n/60, returned $30 of the inventory for a full refund, and received an allowance for $95, how much would the discount be if the retailer remitted payment within the discount window?
$12.50. (Base amount = 750 - 30 - 95 = 625. 2% of 625 = 12.50).
11. LO 6.3
A retailer discovers that 50% of the total inventory items delivered from the manufacturer are damaged. The original purchase for all inventory was $1,100. The retailer decides to return 20% of the damaged inventory for a full refund and keep the remaining 80% of damaged inventory. What is the value of the merchandise returned?
$110. (50% damaged = 550. Return 20% of 550 = 110).
12. LO 6.4
Name two situations where cash would be remitted to a customer from a retailer after purchase.
1) Customer return after payment. 2) Customer allowance after payment.
13. LO 6.4
If a customer purchased merchandise in the amount of $340, terms 3/10, n/30, returned $70 of the inventory for a full refund, and received an allowance for $65, how much discount would be applied if the customer remitted payment within the discount window?
$6.15. (Base = 340 - 70 - 65 = 205. 3% of 205 = 6.15).
14. LO 6.4
A customer discovers 60% of the total merchandise delivered from a retailer is damaged. The original purchase for all merchandise was $3,600. The customer decides to return 35% of the damaged merchandise for a full refund and keep the remaining 65%. What is the value of the merchandise returned?
$756. (Damaged = 3600*0.60=2160. Return = 2160*0.35=756).
15. LO 6.5
What are the main differences between FOB Destination and FOB Shipping Point?
FOB Destination: Seller pays freight, owns goods in transit. FOB Shipping Point: Buyer pays freight, owns goods in transit.
16. LO 6.5
A buyer purchases $250 worth of goods on credit from a seller. Shipping charges are $50. The terms of the purchase are 2/10, n/30, FOB Destination. What, if any, journal entry or entries will the buyer record for these transactions?
Purchase: Dr Inventory $250, Cr AP $250. Shipping: No entry (Seller pays).
17. LO 6.5
A seller sells $800 worth of goods on credit to a customer, with a cost to the seller of $300. Shipping charges are $100. The terms of the sale are 2/10, n/30, FOB Destination. What, if any, journal entry or entries will the seller record for these transactions?
1) Dr AR $800/Cr Sales $800. 2) Dr COGS $300/Cr Inv $300. 3) Dr Delivery Exp $100/Cr Cash $100.
18. LO 6.5
Which statement and where on the statement is freight-out recorded? Why is it recorded there?
Income Statement under Operating Expenses (Selling). It is an expense necessary to complete the sale.
19. LO 6.6
The following is select account information for Sunrise Motors. Sales: $256,400; Sales Returns and Allowances: $34,890; COGS: $120,470; Sales Discounts: $44,760. Given this information, what is the Gross Profit Margin Ratio for Sunrise Motors?
32%. (Net Sales = 176,750. GP = 56,280. Ratio = 56,280/176,750).
20. LO 6.6
What is the difference between a multi-step and simple income statement?
Multi-step distinguishes between operating and non-operating activities and calculates Gross Profit. Simple statement combines all revenues and all expenses.
21. LO 6.6
How can an investor or lender use the Gross Profit Margin Ratio to make financial contribution decisions?
It indicates the percentage of sales available to cover operating expenses and profit. Higher ratios generally indicate efficiency and lower risk.
22. LO 6.6
The following is select account information for August Sundries. Sales: $850,360; Sales Returns and Allowances: $148,550; COGS: $300,840; Operating Expenses: $45,770; Sales Discounts: $231,820. If August Sundries uses a multi-step income statement format, what is their gross margin?
$169,150. (Net Sales 469,990 - COGS 300,840).
23. LO 6.7
If a retailer purchased inventory in the amount of $680, terms 3/10, n/60, returned $120 of the inventory for a full refund, and received an allowance for $70, how much would the discount be if the retailer remitted payment within the discount window?
$14.70. (Base 680-120-70 = 490. 3% of 490 = 14.70).
24. LO 6.7
A customer discovers 50% of the total merchandise delivered from the retailer is damaged. The original purchase for all merchandise was $5,950. The customer decides to return 40% of the damaged merchandise for a full refund and keep the remaining 60%. What is the value of the merchandise returned?
$1,190. (Damaged=2975. Return 40% of 2975 = 1190).
25. LO 6.7
What is the difference in reporting requirements for customer-returned merchandise in sellable condition under a perpetual inventory system versus a periodic inventory system?
Perpetual requires an entry to restore the asset (Dr Inventory, Cr COGS). Periodic does not make an inventory entry at the time of return.

EA 3. LO 6.1 (Matching)
Match each of the following terms with the best corresponding definition.
A. Sales allowance, B. Purchase return, C. Sales discount, D. Purchase discount, E. Sales return, F. Trade discount, G. Purchase allowance.
  • A. Sales allowance: (iii) A customer receives a partial refund but keeps the defective merchandise.
  • B. Purchase return: (vi) A retailer returns merchandise for a full refund.
  • C. Sales discount: (iv) A customer pays their account in full within the discount window.
  • D. Purchase discount: (vii) A retailer pays their account in full within the discount window.
  • E. Sales return: (i) A customer returns merchandise for a full refund.
  • F. Trade discount: (v) A type of purchase discount negotiated between a manufacturer and a retailer before settlement on a final price.
  • G. Purchase allowance: (ii) A retailer receives a partial refund but keeps the defective merchandise.

Answer

TP 1. LO 6.1 (Trade Discounts)
Conduct research on a real-world retailer's trade discounts. Discuss company, products, terms, incentives, and benefits to buyer vs cash discounts.
Guidance Trade discounts are upfront reductions from list price (e.g., for wholesalers), recorded at net price. Cash discounts (2/10) happen at payment. Buyers generally prefer Trade Discounts as they are guaranteed price drops, whereas Cash Discounts require immediate liquidity.
TP 2. LO 6.2 (Convenience Store)
You open a small convenience store. Evaluate Perpetual vs. Periodic inventory systems for this business. Compare benefits/challenges and choose one.
Guidance Recommendation: Perpetual. While Periodic is cheaper, a convenience store has high transaction volume and shrinkage risk (theft). Modern POS systems make Perpetual affordable, allowing real-time tracking of stock levels.
TP 3. LO 6.5 (Invoice)
Draft a standard invoice agreement for customer sales conducted on credit. Include terms, shipping, etc.
Guidance Include: Seller Info, Buyer Info, Date, Invoice #, Item Description, Quantity, Price, Shipping Terms (FOB Shipping Point recommended for high value), Credit Terms (2/10, n/30).
TP 4. LO 6.6 (Income Statement)
Review a public company income statement. Format? Benefits? Compute GP Ratio.
Guidance Most retailers use Multi-Step to show Gross Profit. This helps analysts see markup efficiency separate from overhead costs.
TP 5. LO 6.7 (Clothing Periodic)
Research clothing industry inventory. Why might periodic be used? Advantages?
Guidance Periodic is rarely used in modern chains due to shrinkage visibility issues. It might be used in very small boutiques with manual tags to save on POS software costs.
